###  [Blog1 - Advancing AI agent governance with Boomi and AWS: A unified approach to observability and compliance](3.1-Blog1/)
This blog highlights how Boomi’s Agent Control Tower integrates with Amazon Bedrock to address the growing complexity of managing enterprise AI agents. The solution provides a centralized control plane for total observability, governance, and lifecycle management, enabling organizations to monitor agent performance and enforce security policies across hybrid environments. This approach helps enterprises mitigate "agent sprawl," ensure regulatory compliance, and scale AI operations confidently while optimizing decision-making and operational efficiency.

###  [Blog2 - Scaling your hybrid DNS setup with Amazon Route 53 Resolver endpoint metrics](3.2-Blog2/)
This blog introduces the new ResolverEndpointCapacityStatus metric on Amazon CloudWatch, designed to simplify capacity management for Route 53 Resolver endpoints in hybrid cloud environments. By automatically accounting for factors like latency and protocols to provide a 3-level status alert (Normal, Warning, Critical), this solution enables administrators to make accurate and timely scaling decisions without complex manual analysis.

###  [Blog3 - How the University of Minnesota Athletics built a unified data layer to drive fan engagement with AWS](3.3-Blog3/)
This article details how Minnesota Athletics addressed data fragmentation by building a scalable data lake on AWS. With a lean team of three, they utilized services like Amazon S3, AWS Glue, and AWS Lambda to automate ticketing data processing, resulting in more accurate revenue predictions (3-5% increase) and laying the groundwork for personalized fan engagement through a unified data record.
